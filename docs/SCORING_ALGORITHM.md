## Overview

Lead scoring evaluates businesses based on **detectable signals that logically suggest they might need AI voice agent services** (AI receptionists, booking systems, call handling automation).

**Why Lead Scoring Helps:**
- ‚è±Ô∏è **Saves Time**: Filter leads by characteristics that suggest pain points
- üéØ **Better Context**: Understand WHY a lead was flagged before calling
- üß† **Logical Prioritization**: Start with businesses showing the most opportunity signals
- üìä **Signal Detection**: Automated scanning for pain point indicators

**What We Score:**
- Pain points (no online booking, phone issues)
- Business readiness (established vs struggling)
- Technical gaps (outdated website, no SSL)
- Decision-making ability (independent vs franchise)

**‚ö° Important Note About Modern Tech:**
Even businesses with modern tech (online booking, chat widgets, responsive sites) can upgrade to AI voice agents for:
- 24/7 availability beyond business hours
- Better phone call handling and routing
- More sophisticated automation
- Cost savings vs human receptionists

**Lower scores don't mean "don't contact"** - they mean fewer detectable pain points. You still decide who to reach out to based on your strategy.

## Scoring Philosophy

### Our Approach: Detect Pain Point Indicators

We're looking for businesses that logically might benefit from AI voice agents based on:
1. **Observable operational gaps** (no booking system, phone complaints in reviews)
2. **Business stability indicators** (reviews, ratings suggest established operation)
3. **Absence of competing solutions** (no chat widgets, no modern automation)
4. **Decision-making structure** (independent vs franchise)

### The Logic Behind the Scoring

**Higher Scores (8-10 points) suggest:**
- Multiple detectable pain points that AI could address
- Reviews mentioning phone accessibility issues
- Manual processes (no online booking)
- Established business with resources to invest
- Independent ownership (faster decision-making)

**Lower Scores (0-3 points) suggest:**
- Few or no detectable pain points
- Already invested in automation (chat widgets, booking systems)
- Franchise structure (limited local autonomy)
- Risk factors (very low ratings)

**Important:** These are logical indicators, not guarantees. A low-scored lead might still buy, and a high-scored lead might say no. Sales is complex - the algorithm just helps you spot patterns.

## How Scoring Works

### Base Score
All leads start at **0 points**

### Signal Detection
Our system analyzes each business for specific signals (data points that indicate opportunity or risk)

### Point Accumulation
- **Positive signals** add points (+1, +2, +3)
- **Negative signals** subtract points (-1)
- **Final score** = sum of all signal points

### Score Range
- Minimum: **0 points** (no opportunity)
- Maximum: **10 points** (perfect opportunity)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I customize the scoring algorithm?
Not currently. The scoring algorithm is optimized for AI voice agent sales based on data from successful deals. Future versions may allow custom weighting.

### How often is scoring updated?
Scores are calculated when leads are first generated (during the search). They don't update automatically unless you re-run the search.

### What if a lead has a low score but I know they're interested?
Scores are guidance, not rules. If you have inside information (referral, personal connection, expressed interest), contact them regardless of score.

### Why did my competitor close a 4/10 scored lead?
Scoring predicts probability, not certainty. A 4/10 lead has ~10-15% close rate vs 30-40% for 8+. Lower scores just mean lower odds, not impossible.

### Can I see the exact scoring formula?
Yes! Click any lead score badge in the leads table to see the full breakdown with point-by-point calculation.

### Does the algorithm learn from my wins/losses?
Not yet. This is a planned feature (Success Tracking). Future versions will adjust scores based on your actual close rates.

### What if I disagree with a signal's point value?
The point values are based on aggregated sales data. If you consistently find a signal doesn't matter for your market, let us know! We refine the algorithm based on user feedback.

### Are negative signals dealbreakers?
No. A lead with a chat widget can still buy (upgrade to voice AI). A franchise might have local decision authority. Negative signals just reduce probability, they don't disqualify.

### How do you detect these signals?
Signals are detected by our n8n automation workflow during lead generation:
- Website scraping (SSL, mobile-friendly, copyright year)
- Google Places API (reviews, rating, hours)
- Review text analysis (phone issues, booking mentions)
- Pattern matching (franchise indicators, chat widgets)

### Can I request new signals?
Yes! If you notice patterns in your successful deals that we don't score for, share them. We continuously improve the algorithm.

### What's the difference between score and "Why Flagged"?
- **Score**: Numerical assessment (0-10) based on all signals
- **Why Flagged**: The PRIMARY reason this lead was surfaced (the strongest pain point)

Example: Lead scores 8/10 with "Why Flagged: No booking + phone issues" means those 2 signals (+4 points combined) are the main opportunity.
